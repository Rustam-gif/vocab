# Complete IELTS Vocabulary List - 100 Words

## Academic Life (Set 1)
1. **lecture** - A formal talk given to teach people about a subject
2. **assignment** - A piece of work given to someone as part of their studies
3. **research** - A detailed study to discover new information
4. **semester** - Half of an academic year in schools and universities
5. **deadline** - The latest time by which something must be completed

## Environment & Climate (Set 2)
6. **pollution** - Harmful substances contaminating the environment
7. **sustainable** - Able to continue without harming the environment
8. **ecosystem** - A community of living organisms and their environment
9. **emissions** - Gases or substances released into the atmosphere
10. **renewable** - Natural resources that can be replenished

## Technology & Innovation (Set 3)
11. **artificial** - Made by humans rather than occurring naturally
12. **digital** - Using computer technology and the internet
13. **algorithm** - A set of rules for solving problems in computing
14. **automation** - Using machines to do work without human control
15. **breakthrough** - An important discovery or development

## Health & Medicine (Set 4)
16. **diagnosis** - The identification of an illness or disease
17. **symptom** - A sign that indicates the presence of disease
18. **treatment** - Medical care given to a patient for an illness
19. **prevention** - Actions taken to stop something from happening
20. **immunity** - The body's ability to resist infection

## Business & Economics (Set 5)
21. **profit** - Money gained from business after costs are paid
22. **investment** - Money put into a business to make more money
23. **inflation** - A general increase in prices over time
24. **entrepreneur** - Someone who starts and runs a business
25. **consumer** - A person who buys goods or services

## Government & Politics (Set 6)
26. **legislation** - Laws passed by a government
27. **democracy** - A system where people vote to choose leaders
28. **policy** - A plan or course of action by a government
29. **parliament** - The group of elected representatives who make laws
30. **campaign** - Organized activities to achieve a political goal

## Media & Communication (Set 7)
31. **broadcast** - To send out programs on TV or radio
32. **journalism** - The work of collecting and reporting news
33. **censorship** - Control of what can be published or broadcast
34. **propaganda** - Information used to promote a political cause
35. **editorial** - An article expressing the editor's opinion

## Social Issues (Set 8)
36. **inequality** - Unfair difference between groups in society
37. **poverty** - The state of being extremely poor
38. **discrimination** - Unfair treatment based on characteristics
39. **welfare** - Government support for people in need
40. **diversity** - The inclusion of people from different backgrounds

## Arts & Culture (Set 9)
41. **aesthetic** - Concerned with beauty or appreciation of beauty
42. **exhibition** - A public display of art or other items
43. **contemporary** - Belonging to the present time
44. **heritage** - Traditions and culture passed down generations
45. **masterpiece** - An outstanding work of art or craftsmanship

## Science & Research (Set 10)
46. **hypothesis** - A proposed explanation that can be tested
47. **experiment** - A scientific test to prove or discover something
48. **evidence** - Facts or information that prove something
49. **analysis** - Detailed examination of something
50. **methodology** - A system of methods used in research

## Travel & Tourism (Set 11)
51. **destination** - The place where someone is going
52. **itinerary** - A planned route or journey schedule
53. **accommodation** - A place where people can stay temporarily
54. **hospitality** - Friendly and generous treatment of guests
55. **attraction** - A place of interest that tourists visit

## Food & Agriculture (Set 12)
56. **organic** - Produced without artificial chemicals
57. **nutrition** - The process of getting food for good health
58. **cultivation** - The process of growing plants for food
59. **harvest** - The gathering of mature crops
60. **livestock** - Farm animals raised for food or products

## Urban Development (Set 13)
61. **infrastructure** - Basic systems and services in a city
62. **residential** - Relating to areas where people live
63. **metropolitan** - Relating to a large city and its suburbs
64. **congestion** - Overcrowding causing traffic delays
65. **zoning** - Dividing land into areas for specific uses

## Education System (Set 14)
66. **curriculum** - The subjects taught in a school or course
67. **pedagogy** - The method and practice of teaching
68. **literacy** - The ability to read and write
69. **vocational** - Relating to skills for a particular job
70. **assessment** - The process of evaluating students' knowledge

## Crime & Law (Set 15)
71. **defendant** - A person accused of a crime in court
72. **prosecution** - The process of charging someone with a crime
73. **verdict** - A decision made by a jury in a trial
74. **justice** - Fair treatment according to the law
75. **rehabilitation** - Helping criminals return to normal life

## Psychology & Behavior (Set 16)
76. **cognitive** - Related to mental processes like thinking
77. **motivation** - The reason or desire to do something
78. **perception** - The way something is understood or interpreted
79. **anxiety** - A feeling of worry or nervousness
80. **resilience** - The ability to recover from difficulties

## Global Issues (Set 17)
81. **humanitarian** - Concerned with reducing human suffering
82. **refugee** - Someone forced to leave their country
83. **conflict** - A serious disagreement or war
84. **famine** - Extreme shortage of food affecting many people
85. **pandemic** - A disease outbreak affecting multiple countries

## Sports & Fitness (Set 18)
86. **athletic** - Physically strong and good at sports
87. **endurance** - The ability to continue a difficult activity
88. **competition** - An organized event where people compete
89. **stamina** - Physical strength to sustain prolonged effort
90. **performance** - How well someone does in a sport or activity

## Finance & Banking (Set 19)
91. **mortgage** - A loan to buy property, paid back over years
92. **credit** - The ability to borrow money to be paid later
93. **assets** - Valuable things owned by a person or company
94. **budget** - A plan for spending money over a period
95. **transaction** - A business deal or exchange of money

## Employment & Career (Set 20)
96. **qualification** - Skills or knowledge needed for a job
97. **promotion** - Moving to a higher position at work
98. **resignation** - The act of leaving a job voluntarily
99. **productivity** - The rate at which work is completed
100. **colleague** - A person you work with professionally
